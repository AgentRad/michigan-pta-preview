Accessibility statement.

Michigan PTA is committed to making this website usable by everyone, including people with disabilities. We treat accessibility as core to our mission, not as an add-on.

Our commitment

This site is built to Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA. We do not use accessibility overlays. Accessibility is built into the design and code from the start.

WCAG 2.1 AA Section 508 Aligned No Overlays Keyboard Accessible

What that means in practice

  • Color contrast meets or exceeds 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text and UI components.
  • Keyboard navigation reaches every interactive element. Focus indicators are visible everywhere.
  • Screen readers are supported. Pages use semantic landmarks (header, nav, main, aside, footer) and ARIA where native HTML is insufficient.
  • Skip link at the top of every page jumps past the navigation to the main content.
  • Forms have explicit labels, error messages tied to fields via aria-describedby, and a non-color-only error state.
  • Images have alt text. Decorative images use alt="" so they are skipped by screen readers.
  • Touch targets are at least 44 by 44 pixels on mobile.
  • Reduced motion is honored via prefers-reduced-motion. No animations are required to use any feature.
  • Document language is declared (lang="en") so screen readers pronounce content correctly.

How we test

Every release runs through automated and manual checks. Automated alone is not enough; assistive-technology testing finds what scanners miss.

  • Automated: axe-core, WAVE, Lighthouse Accessibility scoring, Pa11y CI in our deploy pipeline.
  • Manual keyboard: tab order, focus visibility, skip-link, modal traps, form recovery.
  • Screen readers: VoiceOver (macOS, iOS), NVDA (Windows), TalkBack (Android).
  • Color and contrast: WebAIM Contrast Checker, simulated color blindness.
  • Zoom and reflow: 200% browser zoom and 400% with reflow on 1280px viewports.
  • Real users: we invite member volunteers who use assistive technology to test major releases.

Known limitations

Despite our best effort, some content may have minor accessibility issues. As of the most recent audit:

  • Some legacy PDFs predate WCAG 2.1 and may not be fully tagged. We are migrating to HTML or remediated PDFs as part of the rebuild.
  • Embedded third-party content (maps, video players) follows the third party's accessibility standards. We choose vendors who meet WCAG 2.1 AA whenever possible.
  • Spanish-language versions are launching as part of Phase 2.

Report a barrier

If you cannot access information or complete a task on this site, please tell us. We treat accessibility reports as a high priority.

Email: access@michiganpta.org
Response time: within 3 business days. Most issues are fixed in the next release; urgent barriers are patched immediately.

Include in your message: the page URL, what you were trying to do, what happened, and which assistive technology you were using.

Formal complaints

If you are not satisfied with our response, you can file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Justice ADA Information Line or the Michigan Department of Civil Rights.

Standards we follow

  • Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA
  • Section 508 of the U.S. Rehabilitation Act
  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Title III

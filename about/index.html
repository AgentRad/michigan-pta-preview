About Michigan PTA

Michigan's oldest child advocacy nonprofit, born in Battle Creek on May 19, 1918, working today to make every child's potential a reality.

Our mission

Make every child's potential a reality.

The Michigan Parent Teacher Association mobilizes the forces of school, home, and community in order to ensure a quality education and nurturing environment for every child.

We promote local, regional and state efforts focused on the education and well-being of all children in Michigan by working with families, educators, school boards, statewide associations and nonprofit organizations, business and community leaders, the legislature, the State Board of Education and the Michigan Department of Education to ensure child-related concerns are being met.

Our history

Founded by two women when women could not yet vote.

Alice McLellan Birney and Phoebe Apperson Hearst founded the National Congress of Mothers in 1897. They believed mothers would support a mission to eliminate threats that endangered children. Twenty years later, 37 chartered state congresses existed.

Michigan PTA was chartered in Battle Creek on May 19, 1918, becoming one of the earliest state PTAs in the nation. Through every era — child labor reform, school nutrition, mental health, civil rights, school safety — Michigan PTA volunteers have stood with families and educators to make children's lives better.

A century of milestones

  • 1897National Congress of Mothers convenes in Washington, D.C.
  • 1918Michigan PTA chartered in Battle Creek, MI.
  • 1924Renamed to include teachers as full members.
  • 1970sStatewide advocacy for school health and nutrition standards.
  • 2020sMental health, equity, and digital safety initiatives statewide.
What we stand on

Five values, every decision.

Every program, partnership, and policy position runs through these.

Collaboration

Partnership with diverse stakeholders to expand the capacity of our service to children and families.

Commitment

Dedication to educational success, health, and well-being through family and community engagement.

Diversity

Recognition that the potential we serve exists across every demographic, region, and background.

Respect

Valuing the individual contribution of every member, volunteer, and family we serve.

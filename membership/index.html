Three ways to belong.

Local PTA, Great Lakes PTA, or PTSA for students. One voice multiplied by thousands of Michigan families and educators.

Pick the path that fits

Membership types.

Membership is informational on this site. Each path links to the appropriate local or National PTA signup. Michigan PTA does not collect payments through this website.

Local School PTA

The most common path. Join the PTA at your child's school to support the immediate community and have a voice in school decisions.

Who: Parents, guardians, teachers, and staff at any Michigan public school.

Find Your Local PTA

Great Lakes PTA

The state-level membership (formerly A.L.L.) for people who want to support PTA's mission at a broader level than a single school.

Who: Community leaders, grandparents, retired educators, anyone whose impact reaches beyond one school.

Join via National PTA

Student PTSA

Parent Teacher Student Association. For middle and high school PTAs where students are full members alongside families and educators.

Who: Students grade 6-12, parents, guardians, teachers, and staff.

Why join

Your dues fund the work.

PTA dues stay mostly local. A portion supports Michigan PTA advocacy in Lansing and a portion supports National PTA's federal work. Every level matters.

Members also get: regular communication and Advocacy Alerts from Michigan PTA, training and leadership development, the Newsflash newsletter, the Reflections arts program for student members, and connection to a nationwide network of PTA advocates.

Common questions

Membership FAQ.

The questions Michigan families ask most. Don't see yours? Email info@michiganpta.org.

FAQ

How much are dues?

Dues are set by each local PTA, typically $5 to $15 per year. A small portion goes to Michigan PTA and National PTA to fund state and federal advocacy.

FAQ

Do I have to volunteer?

No. Joining as a member supports the work whether you can attend meetings or not. Many members support purely by adding their voice.

FAQ

Can I join if my child's school doesn't have a PTA?

Yes. Join Great Lakes PTA directly through National PTA, or contact us about starting a PTA at your school.
